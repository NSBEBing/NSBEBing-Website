About The National Society of Black Engineers

History

The National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE) was founded in 1975 at Purdue University to increase the number of African American engineers. Established by six members of Purdueâ€™s Black Society of Engineers, NSBE became a 501(c)(3) non-profit in 1976. Today, with over 30,000 members across college, alumni, and pre-college chapters, NSBE's mission is to train Black engineers to excel academically, succeed professionally, and positively impact their communities.

Mission

Our mission is to increase the number of culturally responsible Black engineers who excel academically, thrive professionally, and positively impact their communities. We aim to foster a supportive environment that encourages collaboration, innovation, and leadership development. Through mentorship, professional development opportunities, and community outreach, we strive to empower the next generation of Black engineers to reach their full potential.

Initiatives

We enhance our members' technical and professional skills through workshops on professional etiquette, resume writing, and interview skills. To inspire high school students to explore engineering and science, we launched the Pre-College Initiative (PCI) program. During National Engineers Week, NSBE l eads efforts to raise awareness about engineering among African Americans and other underrepresented groups, proudly serving as the lead organization for this event.
